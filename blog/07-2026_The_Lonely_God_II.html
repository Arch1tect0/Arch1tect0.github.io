The Lonely God...(Part II)

This is the second part where I mainly talk about christinaity.

The Profaned Church

do we really need a faith based organization? Could it be something totally personal? The more we make any organization bigger, the more power and corruption is added to the equation. Now you need a moral baseline for any human political, religious movement so we can't simply take it out of the equation either. As of now I don't have a good answer to whetehr or not we need religious organization and assign so much power to ordinary people.

The God Incarnate

That's the main theme of Christianity. There are so many records of gospels and teachings in early Christianity, but it had to be unified one way or another.

The duality that he was both man and God. Now, there are different ways that we can interpret what it means to be God, and the one that I like goes something like this: "You become so aligned with God, and lose sight of all that is material that you become a mirror that reflects God."

But does it truly make a difference in either case? I beleive it does, and the latter serves the church more and gives them more power.

The God of Kindness and Love

Now if you ask me, I honestly don't knwo and If I have to choose I'd say he was human, a pure one. I honestly couldn't find anything wrong with the actual teachings. But, it does seem a little bit too symbolic and at times reflections of what others had written. I'd say these teachings should be personal and that's the only way that true morality makes sense. It comes from withing one's self and only there.
