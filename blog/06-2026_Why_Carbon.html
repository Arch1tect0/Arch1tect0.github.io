Is 'Carbon Atom' the center of the universe?

I've been wrestling with this idea for a while. Is carbon the main catalyst of life?

It seemed very crazy at first but carbon is capable of creating these complex bonds and that could be in fact the essence of life; Almost every where in the universe. Well, to be a little more accurate, carbon is the way it is because of the conditions in our plant so let's say by the tempreature change, atoms start to act differently but the idea is still the same.

I researched for a while where and how carbon ended up on our beautiful planet and there are several theories for that. I think they are somewhat irrelevant to this conversation though.

So it is Carbon as the backbone structure for life, and then our good old water as the mediator to allow life to become more complex.
